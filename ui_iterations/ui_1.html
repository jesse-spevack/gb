Understanding check result badges

Learn how to interpret plagiarism and AI detection results in your student work assessments.

Badge system overview

Instead of showing exact percentages, we display confidence levels as easy-to-understand badges. This approach helps you quickly identify which submissions need attention while reducing false concerns from minor similarities.

Why badges instead of percentages? Research shows that raw percentages can be misleading and create unnecessary anxiety. Our badge system focuses on actionable insights.

Low confidence

Low

What it means:

Low chance of plagiarism or AI-generated content (0-33% confidence). The submission appears to be original student work.

What to do:

  • • No action typically needed
  • • Focus on providing academic feedback
  • • Proceed with normal grading process

Unclear results

Unclear

What it means:

Uncertain results (34-66% confidence). The system detected some similarities but can't determine if they're problematic.

What to do:

  • • Review the specific content flagged
  • • Consider the assignment context
  • • Use your professional judgment
  • • May warrant a conversation with the student

High confidence

High

What it means:

High chance of plagiarism or AI-generated content (67-100% confidence). Significant similarities detected that require attention.

What to do:

  • • Investigate the flagged content thoroughly
  • • Review assignment guidelines with student
  • • Consider academic integrity policies
  • • Document findings for institutional records

No data available

No data

What it means:

Checks couldn't be performed on this submission. This might happen with certain file formats or technical issues.

What to do:

  • • Verify file format is supported
  • • Check if content is substantial enough
  • • Consider manual review if concerned
  • • Contact support if issues persist

Best practices for educators

Do:

  • ✓ Use badges as a starting point for investigation
  • ✓ Consider the assignment context and requirements
  • ✓ Combine with your professional judgment
  • ✓ Engage students in learning conversations

Don't:

  • ✗ Make decisions based solely on badge results
  • ✗ Assume all similarities are problematic
  • ✗ Skip investigation for "Unclear" results
  • ✗ Ignore the educational opportunity

Frequently asked questions

Why don't you show exact percentages?

Exact percentages can be misleading and create false precision. A 45% match might sound alarming, but could simply indicate proper citations or common academic language. Our badge system focuses on actionable insights.

What should I do if I disagree with a badge result?

Badge results are meant to assist, not replace, your professional judgment. Always review the flagged content in context and make decisions based on your expertise and institutional policies.

How accurate are these checks?

Our detection systems are continuously improving, but no automated system is perfect. Use badges as a helpful tool alongside your teaching experience and knowledge of your students.

Need help interpreting results? Contact your academic integrity office or IT support.
