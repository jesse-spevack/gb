Understanding check result badges

Frequently asked questions about our new confidence-based approach to plagiarism and AI detection results.

Badge types

Low 0-33% confidence
Unclear 34-66% confidence
High 67-100% confidence
No data Check not available

Why did you switch from exact percentages to confidence badges?

Exact percentages can be misleading and create false precision. A score of 47% doesn't necessarily mean more plagiarism than 46%. Our confidence levels give you a clearer understanding of what the results actually mean:

  • Low means we're confident the content is likely original
  • Unclear means the results are ambiguous and need human review
  • High means we're confident there's likely plagiarism or AI content

What should I do when I see an "Unclear" badge?

Unclear results (34-66% confidence) indicate that the detection tools are uncertain. This is actually valuable information - it tells you where human judgment is most needed. Consider:

  • Manually reviewing the flagged sections
  • Looking for patterns in the student's other work
  • Having a conversation with the student if concerns arise
  • Focusing your attention on "High" confidence results first

How reliable are "High" confidence results?

High confidence badges (67-100%) indicate strong evidence of plagiarism or AI content, but they're not definitive proof. These results warrant investigation, but remember:

  • Detection tools can flag legitimate quotes, citations, or common phrases
  • Some writing styles naturally trigger detection algorithms
  • Always review the specific flagged content, not just the badge
  • Use these results as a starting point for academic integrity conversations

Can I still see the exact percentages?

The exact percentages are available in the detailed report view. However, we encourage focusing on the confidence levels for initial screening, as they provide more actionable guidance for your workflow.

What does "No data" mean?

A "No data" badge appears when:

  • The plagiarism or AI detection service is temporarily unavailable
  • The submission format isn't supported by the detection tool
  • There was an error processing the submission
  • The content is too short to analyze effectively

These submissions may need manual review or resubmission.
